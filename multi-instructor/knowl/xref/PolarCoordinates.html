From rectangular to polar
ðŸ”—
Given rectangular coordinates \((x,y)\) of a point \(P\text{,}\) the polar coordinates \((r,\theta)\) of \(P\) satisfy
\begin{align*} r^2 \amp = x^2 + y^2 \amp \tan(\theta) \amp= \frac{y}{x} \text{,} \end{align*}
assuming \(x \neq 0\text{.}\)
