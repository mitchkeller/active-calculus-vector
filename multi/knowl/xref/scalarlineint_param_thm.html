Theorem 13.5.11. Calculating Scalar Line Integrals with Parameterizations.

Let \(C\) be a curve in space parameterized by \(r(t)\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{.}\) If \(f(x,y,z)\) is a multivariable function that is continuous for a region around \(C\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int_C f \, ds =\int_a^b f\left(\vr\left(t\right)\right) |{\vr'(t)}| \, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
