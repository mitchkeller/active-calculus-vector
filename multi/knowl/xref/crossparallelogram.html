Key Idea 9.4.8.

The magnitude of the cross product, \(\vu\times\vv= \Vert\vu\Vert\Vert\vv\Vert\sin(\theta)\text{,}\) is the area of the parallelogram defined by \(\vu\) and \(\vv\text{.}\) Additionally, \(\vu\times\vv\) is the outward direction for the parallelogram defined by \(\vu\) and \(\vv\text{.}\)

Proof.

The area of a parallelogram is the product of its base and height. Based on FigureÂ 9.4.7, we consider the base of the parallelogram to be \(\vecmag{\vu}\) and the height to be \(\vecmag{\vv}\sin(\theta)\text{.}\) This means that the area of the parallelogram formed by \(\vu\) and \(\vv\) is
\begin{equation*} \vecmag{\vu} \vecmag{\vv} \sin(\theta) = \vecmag{\vu\times\vv}\text{.} \end{equation*}
