Both the velocity and acceleration are vector quantities: they have magnitude and direction. In single-variable calculus, we distinguish the velocity of an object, which can be positive, negative, or zero, from its speed, which must be nonnegative. The speed of an object in the single-variable case is the absolute value of its velocity.
