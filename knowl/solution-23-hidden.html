(a) Since \(z=120\) feet when \(t=0\text{,}\) the tower is 120 feet high. (b) The child reaches the bottom when \(z=0\text{,}\) so \(t=120/5=24\) minutes. (c) Her velocity is given by
\begin{equation*} \vec v =\frac{d\vec r}{dt} = -(30\sin t)\vec i +(30\cos t)\vec j - 5\vec k, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Speed} = ||\vec v|| = \sqrt{(-30\sin t)^2+(30\cos t)^2 + (-5)^2} = \sqrt{30^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{925} \mbox{ ft/min}. \end{equation*}
(c) Her acceleration is given by
\begin{equation*} \vec a = \frac{d\vec v}{dt} = -(30 \cos t)\vec i -(30 \sin t)\vec j \mbox{ ft/min}^2. \end{equation*}
