Solution 12.7.6.8.1.

\(\vec G\) has its component in the \(x\)-direction given by
\begin{equation*} (\mbox {curl }\vec G)_x \approx \frac{\mbox {Circulation around small square around } x \mbox {-axis}}{\mbox {Area inside square}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{\mbox {Circulation around }S_2}{\mbox {Area inside }S_2}= \frac{2}{(0.1)^2} = 200. \end{equation*}
Similar reasoning leads to
\begin{equation*} (\mbox {curl }\vec G)_y\approx \frac{\mbox {Circulation around } S_3}{\mbox {Area inside }S_3}=\frac{-0.03}{(0.1)^2}=-3. \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} (\mbox {curl }\vec G)_z\approx\frac{\mbox {Circulation around } S_1}{\mbox {Area inside }S_1}=\frac{4}{(0.1)^2}=400. \end{equation*}
Thus,
\begin{equation*} \mbox {curl }\vec G\approx 200\,\mathit{\vec i}-3\,\mathit{\vec j}+400\,\mathit{\vec k}. \end{equation*}
