Solution 12.3.5.3.1.

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) = \left\lt -4 \sin (2 t^3), 3 \cos (- t^2), 4 t^4 \right> \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{r} '(t) = \left\lt 6 t^2, -2 t, 2 \right > \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) \cdot \mathbf{r} '(t) = \left\lt -4 \sin (2 t^3), 3 \cos (- t^2), 4 t^4 \right> \cdot \left\lt 6 t^2, -2 t, 2 \right > \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =-24 t^2 \sin (2 t^3) - 6 t \cos (- t^2) + 8 t^4 \end{equation*}
The line integral is then:
\begin{equation*} \int_{\mathcal{C}} \mathbf{F} \cdot \,d\ \mathbf{r} = \int^1_0 \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) \cdot \mathbf{r} '(t) \ dt = \int^1_0 -24 t^2 \sin (2 t^3) - 6 t \cos (- t^2) + 8 t^4\ dt \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \left [ 4 \cos (2 t^3) + 3 \sin (- t^2) + \frac{8}{5} t^5 \right ]^1_0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 4 \cos (2) + 3 \sin (-1) + \frac{-12}{5} \end{equation*}
