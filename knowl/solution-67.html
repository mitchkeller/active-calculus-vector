Solution 11.5.4.12.1.

(a) The curve \(r=1/(5\cos\theta)\text{,}\) or \(r\cos\theta=1/5\text{,}\) is the line \(x = 1/5\text{.}\) The curve \(r=1\) is the circle of radius 1 centered at the origin. This gives the graph
The line intersects the circle where \(5\cos\theta=1\text{,}\) so \(\theta = \pm\arccos(1/5)\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Area }=\int_{-\arccos(1/5)}^{\arccos(1/5)}\int_{1/(5\cos\theta)}^1 r\,dr\,d\theta. \end{equation*}
Evaluating gives
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Area }= \int_{-\arccos(1/5)}^{\arccos(1/5)}\left(\frac{r^2}{2}\bigg|_{1/(5\cos\theta)}^1\right)d\theta =\frac{1}{2}\int_{-\arccos(1/5)}^{\arccos(1/5)}\left(1-\frac{1}{25\cos^2\theta}\right)d\theta \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \mbox{Area }=\frac{1}{2}\left(\theta -\frac{\tan\theta}{25}\right) \bigg|_{-\arccos(1/5)}^{\arccos(1/5)} =\frac{1}{2}\left(2(\arccos(1/5)) - 2\frac{\tan(\arccos(1/5))}{25}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \arccos(\frac1{5}) - \frac1{25}\,\tan(\arccos(\frac1{5})). \end{equation*}
