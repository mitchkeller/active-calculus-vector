\begin{equation*} C = 6(hl+wh) + 10 wl. \end{equation*}
But the volume \(= wlh = 510\text{,}\) so \(l = 510/(wh)\text{,}\) thus
\begin{equation*} C = \frac{3060}{w} + 6 wh + \frac{5100}{h}. \end{equation*}
To minimize \(C\text{,}\) we find the critical points of \(C\) by solving
\begin{equation*} C_h = 6 w - \frac{5100}{h^2} = 0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} C_w = 6 h - \frac{3060}{w^2} = 0. \end{equation*}
We get
\begin{equation*} 6 w h^2 = 5100, \quad\mbox{and}\quad 6 h w^2 = 3060, \end{equation*}
Since \(w\text{,}\) \(h \ne 0\text{,}\) we can divide the first equation by the second, giving
\begin{equation*} \frac{6 w h^2}{6 h w^2} = \frac{5100}{3060}, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \frac{h}{w} = \frac{5}{3}, \end{equation*}
thus
\begin{equation*} h = \frac{5}{3} w. \end{equation*}
Substituting this into \(C_w = 0\text{,}\) we obtain \(w^3 = 170\text{,}\) so \(w = 6.73866\) and \(h = 11.2311\) cm. Then \(l = 510/(wh) = 6.73867\) cm. Next we can check that these dimensions minimize the cost C. We find that
\begin{equation*} D=C_{hh}C_{ww}-C^2_{hw} = (\frac{10200}{h^3})(\frac{6120}{w^3}) - 6^2, \end{equation*}
So that at the critical point, \(D > 0\) and \(C_{hh} > 0\text{,}\) and thus \(C\) has a local minimum at \(h=11.2311\) and \(w=6.73866\text{.}\) Since \(C\) increases without bound as \(w,h\to 0\) or \(\infty\text{,}\) this local minimum must be a global minimum. Therefore, the dimensions of the box that minimize the cost are \(w=6.73866\) cm, \(l=6.73867\) cm and \(h=11.2311\) cm.
