\begin{equation*} \mbox{div}\,{\vec{F}} = 4\cdot 2x + 5\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z. \end{equation*}
So, if \(W\) is the interior of the box, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{W}(4\cdot 2x + 5\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z)dV = \int_{3}^{5}\int_{0}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}(4\cdot 2x + 5\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z)dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating through each of the integrals in turn, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{3}^{5}\int_{0}^{3} 4\cdot 2x\,z + 5\cdot 2y\,z + 2z^{2}\bigg|_{z=0}^{z=2}\, dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{3}^{5}\int_{0}^{3} 16 x + 20 y + 8\,dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{3}^{5} 16 x y + 10 y^2 + 8 y\bigg|_{y=0}^{y=3}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{3}^{5} 48 x + 90 + 24\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 24 x^2 + 90 x + 24 x\bigg|_{x=3}^{x=5} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 612. \end{equation*}
Finding flux directly, note that on the faces \(x=5\) and \(x=3\) the normal vectors to the surfaces are \(\vec i\) and \(-\vec i\) respectively, so that \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 4x^{2}\,dy\,dz\) (where \(x\) is evaluated at 5 or 3), and similarly on \(y = 3\) and \(y = 0\text{,}\) and \(z = 2\) and \(z = 0\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 5y^{2}\,dx\,dz\) and \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 2z^{2}\,dx\,dy\text{,}\) respectively. Thus on \(y = 0\) and \(z = 0\) the flux is zero. On the remaining faces, we have (1) On \(x = 5\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}\,100\,dz\,dy = 600\text{;}\) (2) On \(x = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}\,36\,dz\,dy = -216\text{;}\) (3) On \(y = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{3}^{5}\int_{0}^{2}\,45\,dz\,dx = 180\text{;}\) and (4) On \(z = 2\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{3}^{5}\int_{0}^{3}\,8\,dy\,dx = 48\text{.}\) Thus the total flux is the sum of these, \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = 612\text{.}\)
