In Section 9.9, the idea of a flux integral was introduced by looking at how much of a vector field flows through a given section of a surface in space. In particular, the flux integral measured the accumulated amount of the vector field that is orthogonal to the surface (which changes at different points on the surface.) Theorem 9.9.7 shows the best way to calculate the flux integral in terms of a parameterization of the surface. This was an extension of the method for computing the surface area given in [cross-reference to target(s) "S-11-6-Parametric-Surfaces-Surface-Area" missing or not unique].
