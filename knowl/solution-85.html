Solution 11.9.5.8.1.

\(x = s-at\text{,}\) \(y=t\text{.}\) The four edges of \(R\) are
\begin{equation*} y=0, y=14, y=-\frac{7}{12} x, y=-\frac{7}{12} (x-22). \end{equation*}
The change of variables transforms the edges to
\begin{equation*} t=0, t=14, t=-\frac{7}{12} s + \frac{7}{12} at, t=-\frac{7}{12} s + \frac{7}{12} at + \frac{77}{6}. \end{equation*}
These are equations for the edges of a rectangle in the \(st\)-plane if the last two are of the form: \(s=(\hbox{Constant})\text{.}\) This happens when the \(t\) terms drop out, or \(a=\frac{12}{7}\text{.}\) With \(a=\frac{12}{7}\) the change of variables gives
\begin{equation*} \int\int_T \left\vert \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right\vert \,ds\,dt \end{equation*}
over the rectangle
\begin{equation*} T: 0\le t \le 14, 0\le s\le 22. \end{equation*}
The jacobian \(\left\vert\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right\vert\) is
\begin{equation*} \left\vert\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right\vert = \left\vert\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp -\frac{12}{7} \\ 0 \amp 1 \end{array} \right\vert = 1. \end{equation*}
