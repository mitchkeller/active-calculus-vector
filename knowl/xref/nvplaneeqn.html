Key Idea 11.5.5.

A non-vertical plane that goes through the point \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\) can be expressed in the form \(z = z_0 + a(x-x_0) + b(y-y_0)\text{,}\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are constants.

Proof.

As we saw in SectionÂ 9.6, the equation of a plane passing through the point \((x_0, y_0, z_0)\) may be written in the form \(A(x-x_0) + B(y-y_0) + C(z-z_0) = 0\text{.}\) If the plane is not vertical, then \(C\neq 0\text{,}\) and we can rearrange this equation and write \(C(z-z_0) = -A(x-x_0) - B(y-y_0)\) and thus
\begin{align*} z \amp = z_0-\frac{A}{C}(x-x_0) - \frac{B}{C}(y-y_0)\\ \amp = z_0 + a(x-x_0) + b(y-y_0) \end{align*}
where \(a=-A/C\) and \(b=-B/C\text{,}\) respectively.
