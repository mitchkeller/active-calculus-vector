Activity 11.4.3.

We continue to consider the function \(f\) defined by \(f(x,y) = \sin(x) e^{-y}\text{.}\)
(a)
In Figure 11.4.7, we see the trace of \(f(x,y) = \sin(x) e^{-y}\) with \(x\) held constant with \(x = 1.75\) plotted in blue. You can use the slider in Figure 11.4.7 to look at how the slope of the tangent line changes as you vary the \(y\)-coordinate along this trace. Write a couple of sentences that describe whether the slope of the tangent lines to this curve increase or decrease as \(y\) increases. Be sure to pay attention to which direction corresponds to each coordinate increasing.
(b)
Compute \(f_{yy}(x,y)\) and explain how your observations in the previous task is related to the value of \(f_{yy}(1.75,y)\text{.}\) Be sure to address the notion of concavity in your response and be careful to note the directions in which \(x\) and \(y\) are increasing.
(c)
We want to explore what is described by the mixed partial derivative, \(f_{xy}\) and we will focus on the point given by \((x,y)=(1.75,-1.5)\text{.}\) We are going to work through the definition for \(f_{xy} (1.75,-1.5)\) carefully now.
The partial derivative \(f_x(1.75,-1.5)\) measures the slope of the line tangent to the trace given by \(y=-1.5\text{.}\) The tangent line we are examining is changing in the \(x\)-direction. When we look at \(f_{xy}\text{,}\) we are taking the partial derivative of \(f_x\) with respect to \(y\text{,}\) \(\frac{\partial}{\partial y} \Bigl[ f_x \Bigr]\text{.}\) So we are looking at how the slope of the tangent line in the \(x\)-direction will change when we vary the \(y\)-coordinate a little bit. We would approximate \(f_{xy}(1.75,-1.5)\) with the following difference quotient:
\begin{equation*} \frac{f_x(1.75,-1.5+h)-f_x(1.75,-1.5)}{h} \end{equation*}
You might think of sliding your pencil down the trace with \(x=1.5\) so that the pencil is in the \(x\)-direction and tangent to the surface. In Figure 11.4.8, the pencil would be the red tangent line drawn. You can use the slider to change the \(y\)-coordinate of the point where the red tangent line is drawn. You should look at what happens to the slope of the red tangent line as you increase the \(t\)-cooridnate with the slider. The tangent line at the point \((1.75,-1.5)\) is drawn in gray for reference purposes.
Based on Figure 11.4.8, write a few sentences about whether \(f_{xy}(1.75, -1.5)\) is positive or negative and justify your reasoning.
(d)
Compute \(f_{xy}(x,y)\) and evaluate \(f_{xy}(1.75, -1.5)\text{.}\) Write a couple of sentences about how does this value compare with your observations in the previous task.
(e)
We know that \(f_{xx}(1.75, -1.5)\) measures the concavity of the \(y = -1.5\) trace, and that \(f_{yy}(1.75, -1.5)\) measures the concavity of the \(x = 1.75\) trace. What do you think the quantity \(f_{xy}(1.75, -1.5)\) measures?
