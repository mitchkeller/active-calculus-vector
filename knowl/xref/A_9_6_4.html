Activity 10.1.4.

For this activity we will consider the paraboloid defined by \(z = x^2+y^2\text{.}\)
(a)
Find a parameterization for the intersection of the paraboloid with the \(x=2\) fundamental plane. What type of curve does this intersection describe?
(b)
Find a parameterization for the paraboloid and the \(y = -1\) fundamental plane. What type of curve does this intersection describe?
(c)
Find a parameterization for the intersection of the paraboloid with the fundamental plane \(z = 25\text{.}\) What type of curve does this intersection describe?
(d)
How do your responses change to all three of the preceding questions if you instead consider the surface defined by \(z = x^2 - y^2\text{?}\)
Hint.
For generating one of the parameterizations: \(\sec^2(t)-\tan^2(t) = 1\text{.}\)
