Preview Activity 11.5.1.

We want to find the equation of the plane (of the form given in Key Idea 11.5.5) that best describes the surface given by \(z=f(x,y)=6-\frac{x^2}2 - y^2\text{,}\) around the input \((x_0,y_0) = (1,1)\text{.}\) In particular, we will need to find the proper values of \(z_0\text{,}\) \(a\text{,}\) and \(b\text{.}\)

(a)

Evaluate \(f(x,y) = 6 - \frac{x^2}2 - y^2\) and its partial derivatives at \((x_0,y_0)\text{;}\) that is, find \(f(1,1)\text{,}\) \(f_x(1,1)\text{,}\) and \(f_y(1,1)\text{.}\)

(b)

The value \(z_0\) from Key Idea 11.5.5 will need to be the \(z\)-coordinate value where our plane will intersect the surface. What is the \(z\)-coordinate of the point where the plane and the surface intersect?

(c)

If our plane will best describe the surface near our input \((x_0,y_0)\text{,}\) then the plane will also fit the behavior of the traces \(x=1\) and \(y=1\) near our point of interest.
Sketch the traces of \(f(x,y) = 6 - \frac{x^2}2 - y^2\) for \(y=y_0=1\) and \(x=x_0=1\) below in Figure 11.5.6. Draw the tangent lines to the each of the traces when the input (either \(x\) or \(y\) as appropriate) is 1.

(d)

Give the slopes of the tangent lines to the traces that you drew in the previous part and write a few sentences to explain why the tilt of the tangent plane in the \(x\)-direction is given by the partial derivative of \(f_x\) and the tilt of the tangent plane in the \(y\)-direction is given by the partial derivative of \(f_y\text{.}\) You will likely want to talk about how each of these slopes/partial derivatives relates to the traces (of the surface and the plane) in Figure 11.5.7.
