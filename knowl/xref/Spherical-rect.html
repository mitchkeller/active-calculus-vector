Coverting between rectangular and spherical coordinates.

From rectangular to spherical
Given rectangular coordinates \((x,y,z)\) of a point \(P\text{,}\) the spherical coordinates \((\rho,\theta,\phi)\) of \(P\) satisfy
\begin{align*} \rho \amp = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} \\ \tan(\theta) \amp = \frac{y}{x}\\ \cos(\phi) \amp = \frac{z}{\rho} \end{align*}
where in the second and third equations, we require \(x \ne 0\) and \(\rho \ne 0\text{.}\)
From spherical to rectangular
Given spherical coordinates \((\rho,\theta,\phi)\) of a point \(P\text{,}\) the rectangular coordinates \((x,y,z)\) of \(P\) satisfy
\begin{align*} x \amp = \rho \sin(\phi) \cos(\theta) \\ y \amp = \rho \sin(\phi) \sin(\theta) \\ z \amp = \rho \cos(\phi)\text{.} \end{align*}
