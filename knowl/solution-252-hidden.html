\begin{equation*} \mbox{div}\,{\vec{F}} = 4\cdot 2x + 3\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z. \end{equation*}
So, if \(W\) is the interior of the box, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{W}(4\cdot 2x + 3\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z)dV = \int_{3}^{4}\int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}(4\cdot 2x + 3\cdot 2y + 2\cdot 2z)dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating through each of the integrals in turn, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{3}^{4}\int_{0}^{1} 4\cdot 2x\,z + 3\cdot 2y\,z + 2z^{2}\bigg|_{z=0}^{z=1}\, dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{3}^{4}\int_{0}^{1} 8 x + 6 y + 2\,dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{3}^{4} 8 x y + 3 y^2 + 2 y\bigg|_{y=0}^{y=1}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{3}^{4} 8 x + 3 + 2\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 4 x^2 + 3 x + 2 x\bigg|_{x=3}^{x=4} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 33. \end{equation*}
Finding flux directly, note that on the faces \(x=4\) and \(x=3\) the normal vectors to the surfaces are \(\vec i\) and \(-\vec i\) respectively, so that \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 4x^{2}\,dy\,dz\) (where \(x\) is evaluated at 4 or 3), and similarly on \(y = 1\) and \(y = 0\text{,}\) and \(z = 1\) and \(z = 0\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 3y^{2}\,dx\,dz\) and \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 2z^{2}\,dx\,dy\text{,}\) respectively. Thus on \(y = 0\) and \(z = 0\) the flux is zero. On the remaining faces, we have (1) On \(x = 4\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}\,64\,dz\,dy = 64\text{;}\) (2) On \(x = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{1}\int_{0}^{1}\,36\,dz\,dy = -36\text{;}\) (3) On \(y = 1\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{3}^{4}\int_{0}^{1}\,3\,dz\,dx = 3\text{;}\) and (4) On \(z = 1\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{3}^{4}\int_{0}^{1}\,2\,dy\,dx = 2\text{.}\) Thus the total flux is the sum of these, \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = 33\text{.}\)
