Solution 12.10.4.1.1.

\(x=2 u+9\text{,}\) \(y=u-v\text{,}\) and \(z=3 u+v\) satisfy the equation of the plane,
\begin{equation*} 2x-y-z =2(2 u+9 )-( u-v )-( 3 u+v )= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 4 u+18-u+v-3 u-v=18 \end{equation*}
Moreover, for any \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(z\) satisfying \(2x-y-z=18\text{,}\) there are values of \(u\) and \(v\) such that \(x=2 u+9\text{,}\) \(y=u-v\text{,}\) and \(z=3 u+v\text{,}\) since the following equations can be solved for \(u\) and \(v\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl}x\amp {}={}\amp 2 u+9\\ \noalign{\smallskip} y\amp =\amp u-v\\ \noalign{\smallskip} z\amp =\amp 3 u+v\\ \noalign{\smallskip} 2x-y-z\amp =\amp 18\end{array}\quad\Rightarrow\quad u=\frac{x-9}{2},\quad v=\frac{x-9}{2}-y \end{equation*}
We conclude that \(\mathrm{\Phi }( u,v )\) parametrizes the whole plane \(2x-y-z=18\text{.}\) (a) The tangent vectors \({\mathbf{T} }_u\) and \({\mathbf{T} }_v\) are:
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf{T} }_u =\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial u}=\frac{\partial }{\partial u}( 2 u+9,u-v,3 u+v )= \left\lt 2,1,3 \right> \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf{T} }_v =\frac{\partial \phi }{\partial v}=\frac{\partial }{\partial v}( 2 u+9,u-v,3 u+v )= \left\lt 0,-1,1 \right> \end{equation*}
The normal vector is the following cross product:
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{n} ( u,v )={\mathbf{T} }_u \times {\mathbf{T} }_v = \left|\begin{array}{rrr}\mathbf{i} \amp \mathbf{j} \amp \mathbf{k} \\ 2\amp 1\amp 3\\ 0\amp -1\amp 1\end{array}\right|= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \left|\begin{array}{rr}1\amp 3\\ -1\amp 1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{i} -\left|\begin{array}{rr}2\amp 3\\ 0\amp 1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{j} +\left|\begin{array}{rr}2\amp 1\\ 0\amp -1\end{array}\right|\mathbf{k}= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 4 \mathbf{i} -2 \mathbf{j} -2 \mathbf{k} = \left\lt 4,-2,-2 \right> \end{equation*}
(b) That area of \(S=\mathrm{\Phi }(\mathcal{D})\) is the following surface integral:
\begin{equation*} Area(S)=\iint_{\mathcal{D}} \| \mathbf{n} ( u,v )\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \iint_{\mathcal{D}} \| \left\lt 4,-2,-2 \right>\| \,du \,dv= 2\sqrt{6}\iint_{\mathcal{D}} 1 \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2\sqrt{6}\, \mathrm{Area} (\mathcal{D}) =2\sqrt{6}\cdot 9\cdot 4=72 \sqrt{6} \end{equation*}
(c) We express \(f( x,y,z )=yz\) in terms of the parameters \(u\) and \(v\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} f\left(\phi ( u,v )\right)=( u-v )(3 u+v )=3 u^2-2 uv- v^2 \end{equation*}
Using the Theorem on Surface Integrals we have:
\begin{equation*} \iint_S f( x,y,z ) \,dS = \iint_{\mathcal{D}}f\left(\phi ( u,v )\right)\| \mathbf{n} ( u,v )\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \iint_{\mathcal{D}} \left(3 u^2-2uv- v^2\right)\| \left\lt 4,-2,-2 \right>\| \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2\sqrt{6}\int_0^{4} \int_0^{9} \left(3 u^2-2uv- v^2\right) \,du \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2\sqrt{6}\int_0^{4} \left( \frac{3 u^3}3 -\frac{2u^2v}2- v^2u\right)\bigg|_{u=0}^{9} \,dv= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2\sqrt{6}\int_0^{4} \left(\frac{2187}3-\frac{162v}2-9 v^2\right) \,dv = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2\sqrt{6}\left(\frac{2187v}3-\frac{162v^2}4-\frac{9 v^3}3 \right)\bigg|_0^{4} =\frac{49824\sqrt{6}}{12} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \approx 10170.3 \end{equation*}
