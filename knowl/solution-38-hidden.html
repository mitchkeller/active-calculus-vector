(a) In the direction of \(\nabla F\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \nabla F \bigg|_{(1,1,1)} = \left((4x+8xz^{2})\vec i + 8y^{3} \vec j + 8x^{2}z \vec k \right)\bigg|_{(1,1,1)}= 12\vec i + 8 \vec j + 8 \vec k. \end{equation*}
(b) The rate of change in the direction of \(\nabla F\) with respect to distance \(= ||\nabla F|| = \sqrt{272}\text{.}\) Now we want rate of change with respect to time. If we move at 6 cm/sec:
\begin{equation*} \mbox{Rate of change of } \frac{\mbox{Conc}}{\mbox{Time}} = \mbox{Rate of change of } \frac{\mbox{Conc}}{\mbox{Dist}} \times \mbox{Rate of change of } \frac{\mbox{Dist}}{\mbox{Time}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 6 \sqrt{(272)} \mbox{ mg/cm}^3/\mbox{sec}. \end{equation*}
