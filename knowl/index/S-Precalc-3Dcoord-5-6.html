When we make the constant 0, we get the coordinate planes themselves . The \(xy\)-plane satisfies \(z=0\text{,}\) the \(xz\)-plane satisfies \(y=0\text{,}\) and the \(yz\)-plane satisfies \(x=0\) (see FigureÂ 9.1.7). Planes of the form \(x=a\text{,}\) \(y=b\text{,}\) \(z=c\) are called fundamental planes are useful in understanding and building structures in three dimensions. You can see how the intersection of fundamental planes \(x=1\text{,}\) \(y=-2\text{,}\) and \(z=3\) corresponds to the point \((1,-2,3)\) in FigureÂ 9.1.12.
