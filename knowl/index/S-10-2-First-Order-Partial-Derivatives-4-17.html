Definition 11.3.6.

The first-order partial derivatives of \(f\) with respect to \(x\) and \(y\) at a point \((a,b)\) are (provided the limits exist), respectively,
\begin{align*} f_x(a,b) \amp = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h,b)-f(a,b)}{h}\\ f_y(a,b) \amp = \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \amp = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a,b+h)-f(a,b)}{h} \end{align*}
