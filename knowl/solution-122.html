Solution 12.2.6.3.1.

\(\int_{C_1}\vec F\cdot d\vec r \lt 0\text{,}\) because the curve moves from larger to smaller \(x\)-values, and the vector field points from right to left. Applying this logic to each of the curves, we find that
\begin{equation*} \int_{C_{1}}\vec F\cdot\vec r \lt \int_{C_{2}}\vec F\cdot\vec r \lt \int_{C_{3}}\vec F\cdot\vec r. \end{equation*}
