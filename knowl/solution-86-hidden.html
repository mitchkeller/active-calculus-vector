\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccc} x \amp = \amp 4 s + t\\ y \amp = \amp s - t, \end{array} \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right| = \left|\begin{array}{cc} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s} \amp \frac{\partial x}{\partial t}\\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial s} \amp \frac{\partial y}{\partial t} \end{array}\right| =\left|\begin{array}{cc} 4 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp -1 \end{array}\right| = |-5|, \end{equation*}
hence
\begin{equation*} \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right| = 5. \end{equation*}
We therefore get
\begin{equation*} \int_R (x+y) \,dA = \int_{T} ((4 s + t) + (s - t)) \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(s,t)}\right|dsdt = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \int_{T}(5s)(5)\,ds\,dt=\int_{T} (5\cdot 5s)\,ds\,dt, \end{equation*}
where \(T\) is the region in the \(st\)-plane corresponding to \(R\text{.}\) Now, we need to find \(T\text{.}\) Because
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccc} x \amp = \amp 4 s+ t \\ y \amp = \amp s - t \end{array} \end{equation*}
we can use the boundaries of the region \(R\) to find the boundaries of \(T\text{.}\) Using the four vertices of the parallelogram \(R\text{,}\) we the edges \(y = \frac1{4}\,x\text{,}\) \(y = -x\text{,}\) \(y = \frac{1}{4}\,(x-2) - 2\) and \(y = -(x - 8) + 2\text{.}\) So, from the above transformation, the boundaries are \(t = 0\text{,}\) \(s = 0\text{,}\) \(t = 2\) and \(s = 2\text{.}\) Therefore
\begin{equation*} \int_R(x+y)\,dA = \int_{0}^{2} \int_{0}^{2}\, 5\cdot 5s \,ds\,dt = 100. \end{equation*}
(Alternately, we could of course also write the integral as \(\int_{2}^{0} \int_{2}^{0}\, 5\cdot 5s \,ds\,dt\text{.}\))
