Solution 12.12.4.4.1.

\begin{equation*} \mbox{div}\,{\vec{F}} = 5\cdot 2x + 2\cdot 2y + 5\cdot 2z. \end{equation*}
So, if \(W\) is the interior of the box, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{W}(5\cdot 2x + 2\cdot 2y + 5\cdot 2z)dV = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{3}^{6}\int_{0}^{2}(5\cdot 2x + 2\cdot 2y + 5\cdot 2z)dz\,dy\,dx. \end{equation*}
Integrating through each of the integrals in turn, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{S}\vec{F} \cdot d\vec{A} = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{3}^{6} 5\cdot 2x\,z + 2\cdot 2y\,z + 5z^{2}\bigg|_{z=0}^{z=2}\, dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{3}^{6} 20 x + 8 y + 20\,dy\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{0}^{3} 20 x y + 4 y^2 + 20 y\bigg|_{y=3}^{y=6}\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int_{0}^{3} 60 x + 108 + 60\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 30 x^2 + 108 x + 60 x\bigg|_{x=0}^{x=3} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 774. \end{equation*}
Finding flux directly, note that on the faces \(x=3\) and \(x=0\) the normal vectors to the surfaces are \(\vec i\) and \(-\vec i\) respectively, so that \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 5x^{2}\,dy\,dz\) (where \(x\) is evaluated at 3 or 0), and similarly on \(y = 6\) and \(y = 3\text{,}\) and \(z = 2\) and \(z = 0\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 2y^{2}\,dx\,dz\) and \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \pm 5z^{2}\,dx\,dy\text{,}\) respectively. Thus on \(x = 0\) and \(z = 0\) the flux is zero. On the remaining faces, we have (1) On \(x = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{3}^{6}\int_{0}^{2}\,45\,dz\,dy = 270\text{;}\) (2) On \(y = 6\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}\,72\,dz\,dx = 432\text{;}\) (3) On \(y = 3\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{0}^{2}\,18\,dz\,dx = -108\text{;}\) and (4) On \(z = 2\text{,}\) \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = \int_{0}^{3}\int_{3}^{6}\,20\,dy\,dx = 180\text{.}\) Thus the total flux is the sum of these, \(\vec F\cdot d\vec A = 774\text{.}\)
